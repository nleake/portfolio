Netherlands Embassy

2015 - 2016

The Embassy of the Netherlands wanted a redesigned website to grow business with the United States. The embassy had a blog and social media accounts they wanted to leverage, but the legacy site was difficult to navigate.

  • Goal

    Redesign website to increase visitor engagement. Empower clients to publish new content regularly.

  • Challenge

    How might we highlight innovations from the Netherlands while increasing traffic to blogs and social media accounts?

  • The Team

    One project manager, two graphic designers, and two developers.

  • Approach

    Interview stakeholders to scope the project. Create moodboards to capture desired look and feel for the new site. Use card sorting and existing content to create sitemaps and Sketch prototypes.

  • My Role

    • Manage scope and facilitate workshops with clients.
    • Work with graphic designers to create prototypes and illustrations.
    • Use mocks to write user stories and acceptance test delivered features.

Project Kickoff

We interviewed embassy staff to learn more about their mission and the desired project outcomes. After researching embassy sites and social media accounts, we workshopped moodboards with the client to find a look that matched their branding guidelines and used card sorting to organize site content.

Creating Mocks

We took categories from our card sort to build a sitemap and Balsamiq mockups to illustrate a simpler site navigation. This sectioned the site based on types of innovation from the Netherlands.

After agreeing on the structure for the home page, sections, and article pages, I created high fidelity prototypes in Sketch to illustrate what a finished product might look like.

Measuring Success

Once we had the main pages built and analytics in place, we trained embassy staff to manage site content and monitor performance.

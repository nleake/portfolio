USA TODAY

2013 - 2015

Gannett owned over one hundred newspapers and television stations, each with their own websites. The company supported several case management systems (CMS), some of which lacked basic digital publishing features. USA Today is Gannett's largest property, but their error-prone system was notorious in the newsroom for causing delays.

  • Goal

    Create a single CMS for Gannett news sites.

  • Challenge

    How might we build an intuitive system that could be used by all Gannett properties?

  • The Team

    We were eight developers, two product managers, and one designer.

  • Approach

    Interview journalists to learn how they work and usability test interactive prototypes.

  • My Role

    • Design and prioritize features
    • Write user stories
    • Facilitate interviews and usability tests
    • Perform acceptance testing on delivered stories

User Research

We observed and interviewed dozens of journalists around the country to design a story editor with an inviting interface and intuitive formatting options that would allow journalists to publish breaking news more quickly.

Adding Photos

Our team designed a simple image upload service that could tag and process large collections of images. We used an assumptions tracker to usability test how well journalists understood the interface.

Outcome

After releasing the CMS to the USA TODAY newsroom, it was adopted by Gannett properties nationwide. This later helped enable content sharing across the largest local news network in the United States.
